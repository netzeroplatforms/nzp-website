From perpetual liability
to commercial asset.

NZP converts legacy waste streams — coal spoil, IBA, PFAS — into syngas, recoverable metals and vitrified slag, using ultra-high temperature submerged arc gasification. The feedstock comes to us with payment attached. The operator exits the liability entirely.

The problem operators are paying to ignore

Coal operators, municipal incinerators and industrial sites across the US, UK and Europe are carrying open-ended environmental liabilities — acid mine drainage treatment at $500k–$5m+ per site per year, Clean Water Act penalties at up to $60,000/day/violation, SMCRA bonding shortfalls, IBA disposal gate fees, PFAS stockpile obligations. These are not one-off fines. They are perpetual, compounding costs with no natural exit.

Regulators are tightening. ESG litigation is rising. The operators cannot grow their way out. They need a credible balance sheet solution — not a technology pitch.

What NZP does

  • Structures a liability transfer agreement — NZP takes the feedstock obligation off the operator's balance sheet
  • Converts the waste stream into clean syngas (50–55% H₂, 45–50% CO), recoverable metals and vitrified inert slag
  • The operator's existing financial reserves held against the liability serve as project working capital
  • Outputs sold into contracted offtake — grid-injectable SNG, hydrogen, or SAF via Fischer-Tropsch

What this means for the operator

  • Open-ended liability capped and removed from the balance sheet
  • Ongoing penalty and treatment costs eliminated
  • Financial reserves previously locked against the liability released
  • Regulatory exposure transferred to NZP under a structured SPV/JV
  • No capital outlay required — the liability reserve is the working capital

Why the technology works — submerged arc, not torch-based

The plasma gasification failures investors remember (Solena — bankrupt 2018, lower-temperature torch systems) used external arc torches: energy-hungry, unstable at scale. NZP uses submerged arc technology — the arc is stabilised inside a molten bath, the same physics as 200+ commercial steel-making furnaces. 10,000°C operating temperature. Feedstock variability irrelevant. 200+ systems deployed globally across Asia, Europe and North America over 25+ years. TRL 8–9 — commercial systems, not lab demos. Validated by US EPA, California IWMB, Los Angeles and Dallas-Fort Worth.

Questions operators and investors will ask

Will regulators accept this?
Submerged arc vitrification produces inert, non-leaching slag — classified non-hazardous. NZP's process is validated by US EPA and California IWMB. High-temperature treatment is an established regulatory pathway.
Is the technology proven at scale?
200+ commercial systems deployed over 25+ years. PCB destruction in Taiwan. Mixed waste in Korea. TRL 8–9. Not a pilot project — the same supply chain as the global steel industry.
Who funds deployment?
The operator's existing reserves held against the liability serve as working capital. NZP's Model 1–3 structure means no capital is committed until commercial viability is proven.
Who owns the risk in 20 years?
SPV/JV structure. The liability transfers into the vehicle — it does not remain on the operator's books. NZP takes operational responsibility for the feedstock stream.
