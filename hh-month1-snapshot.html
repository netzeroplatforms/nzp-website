Multiplex Lateral Flow — IP & Commercial Strategy
ALS engagement extended. The ALS reactivation programme (original contact: Tim Lumner) has been broadened into a structured market engagement strategy for HH Global's UK patent-pending Multiplex Lateral Flow technology — true multiplexing of 20-60 proteins/antibodies on a single strip, a capability not yet commercially available at scale. Research confirms no dominant product exists in this category, validating the patent timing. Twelve named companies have been identified across sell-to, partner-with, and manufacturing categories.
Abingdon Health
UK Diagnostics CDMO · AIM-Listed
Priority First Call
York-based AIM-listed UK lateral flow CDMO with an active multiplex lateral flow programme and a live ~$2M multiplex test development contract. ISO 13485 certified. Operates a co-development model with external IP holders — the most natural first conversation in the UK for HH Global to explore strip manufacturing and joint commercialisation of the patent-pending technology.
Next step: Initiate contact to explore co-development and manufacturing partnership for the Multiplex Lateral Flow strip.
Randox Laboratories
UK Diagnostics · 204 Patents
Sell-To / License
Northern Ireland-based major UK diagnostics company. Their Biochip Array Technology detects up to 100 markers from a single sample — the same multiplexing goal as HH Global's strip, but requiring expensive lab analysers. A lateral flow strip delivering equivalent multiplexing at point-of-care cost directly extends their market. With 204 patents and acquisition activity, Randox are the most natural UK licensing or acquisition target. Freedom-to-operate review against their IP portfolio is recommended before approach.
Next step: IP landscape review; identify the right door into Randox's innovation/BD team.
QuidelOrtho
US Diagnostics · LF Commercial Scale
Sell-To
Formed by the $6B merger of Quidel and Ortho Clinical Diagnostics. Actively filing patents on multiplex lateral flow strip formats (including a 20-channel structured substrate design for Lyme disease antibody detection). Their QUICKVUE brand is a major LF product family. A natural commercial acquirer or licensee of a novel UK multiplex strip patent, particularly for infectious disease or autoimmune panel applications.
Next step: Monitor patent filings; identify licensing approach once HH Global IP position is secured.
Manufacturing ecosystem identified. SCIENION AG (Germany) and Axxin Pty Ltd (Australia) have a validated joint platform for microarray-spotted lateral flow strips with reader capability up to 20+ parameters per strip. Future Diagnostics (Netherlands) offers CDMO services specifically for multiplex microarray IVD development. These form a credible manufacturing pathway from HH Global's patent to a commercial product. Full company landscape (12 named entities including sell-to, partner, CDMO and IP risk) available on request.
