Submerged Arc — Not Torch-Based. That Distinction Is the Moat.

Most plasma gasification failures used external torch-based systems — high energy draw, electrode instability, narrow feedstock tolerance. NZP uses submerged arc furnace technology: the arc is stabilised within a molten bath, the same architecture as the global steel industry's electric arc furnaces. The supply chain is commercially mature, globally available, and not dependent on a single supplier. This is not a novel technology — it is proven heavy industry, applied to waste.

Global FMCG Consortium — Feedstock Modelling Underway

We are working with a global consortium of FMCG producers to recover commercial value from food and beverage manufacturing waste streams — including spent grain from beer production, dairy and ice cream waste, and other high-volume organic byproducts. Real feedstock data. Real modelled economics. Across multiple sectors simultaneously.
